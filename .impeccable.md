## Design Context

### Users
Potential freelance clients (hiring managers, CTOs, business leaders) who need data science expertise. They're scanning quickly for credibility, competence, and approachability. They arrive skeptical and need to be convinced in under 10 seconds that this person is worth emailing.

### Brand Personality
Calm, technical, human. Not flashy, not cold, not generic. The restraint is the statement — a confident professional who doesn't need to shout. Like a well-typeset research paper: precise but inviting.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Tone**: Quietly confident. Dark-dominant with tonal blacks, warm off-white text, and a single amber/gold accent for the "human" warmth.
- **Theme**: Light + dark toggle. Dark is default (user preference for black tones). Light mode uses warm whites with the same structural logic.
- **Typography**: Heading: Bricolage — a modern grotesque with subtle personality quirks that reads as technical but not sterile. Body: Libre Franklin — clean, readable, professional pairing.
- **Layout**: Asymmetric, editorial. Left-aligned content with generous whitespace. No centered-everything. Strong typographic hierarchy with stark size contrast.
- **Reference**: Clean card-based portfolio (hacks-deep-63863918.figma.site) — but with more editorial personality and less templating.
- **Anti-references**: No generic tech-blue dashes, no gradient text, no side-stripe borders on cards, no hero metric layout, no centered-everything layouts, no glassmorphism.

### Design Principles
1. **Restraint as strength** — Every element earns its place. White space is a feature, not waste.
2. **Technical precision** — Tight alignment, consistent spacing scale, mathematical type scale. The craft should be invisible but felt.
3. **Warmth through detail** — Amber accent, rounded corners where appropriate, human microcopy. Technical without being sterile.
4. **Bilingual clarity** — EN and PT-BR coexist naturally. The language toggle feels native, not bolted on.
5. **Trust through structure** — Clear hierarchy guides the eye to the CTA. Information architecture builds confidence.
